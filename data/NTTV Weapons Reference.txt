[WEAPON] Hanbo
ALIASES: hanbo, hanbō, short staff, three-foot staff, 3-foot staff
TYPE: Short staff (approx. 90 cm or 3 feet)
KAMAE: Munen Muso, Kata Yaburi, Ontonashi
CORE ACTIONS: shomen uchi, yokomen uchi, age uchi, do-kote strikes, tsuki thrust, ashi-barai sweep
RANKS: Introduced at 8th Kyu
NOTES: Often used to enter, disrupt balance, and strike at skeletal targets; integrates smoothly with taijutsu footwork.

[WEAPON] Rokushakubo
ALIASES: rokushakubo, rokushaku bo, bo, long staff, rokushaku-bō
TYPE: Long staff (approx. 180 cm or 6 feet)
KAMAE: hira no kamae, chudan, jodan, gedan, seigan, heito, ihen
CORE ACTIONS: long-range shomen/yokomen/age/do strikes, tsuki thrusts, sweeping actions, bo-furi spinning drills
RANKS: Introduced at 6th Kyu
NOTES: Emphasizes distance, leverage, and centerline control; integrates ukemi and spinning transitions.

[WEAPON] Shuriken
ALIASES: shuriken, bo shuriken, bō shuriken, senban, shaken, throwing spike, throwing star
TYPE: Throwing blades (spikes or flat blades)
MODES: daken-jutsu (throwing), shoken-jutsu (in-hand striking)
THROWS: jikidaho (direct), hantendaho (reverse), takai-tendaho (fast spin)
RANGE: Common 3-ken range (~5.4 m); extended range develops with practice
SAFETY/DRILL: anchor point alignment, wrist snap, concealment, controlled retrieval path
RANKS: Introduced at 3rd Kyu
NOTES: Used for distraction, pain compliance, and precision targeting; multiple shapes exist across Bujinkan schools.

[WEAPON] Katana
ALIASES: katana, sword, daito
TYPE: Long sword
KAMAE: daijodan, chudan, gedan, seigan, jodan
CORE ACTIONS: kirioroshi (vertical cut), yokogiri (horizontal cut), thrusts, deflections, kamae transitions
RANKS: Introduced at 7th Kyu
NOTES: Movement emphasizes distance, timing, and angling; integrates naturally with taijutsu footwork.

[WEAPON] Tanto
ALIASES: tanto, dagger, knife
TYPE: Short blade (close-range)
CORE ACTIONS: tsuki thrust, yokomen slashes, grip transitions, disarms
RANKS: Introduced at 5th Kyu
NOTES: Often taught as both offensive and defensive material; integrates with taihen (body evasion).

[WEAPON] Shoto
ALIASES: shoto, short sword, shōtō
TYPE: Short sword
CORE ACTIONS: close-range cuts, thrusts, traps, paired weapon interactions
RANKS: Introduced at 5th Kyu
NOTES: Used for confined-space movement and rapid transitions.

[WEAPON] Kusari Fundo
ALIASES: kusari fundo, manriki-gusari, weighted chain
TYPE: Chain weapon with weighted ends
CORE ACTIONS: wrapping, entangling, joint targeting, percussive strikes, weapon control
RANKS: Introduced at 4th Kyu
NOTES: Requires strong control and awareness; blends striking and grappling concepts.

[WEAPON] Kyoketsu Shoge
ALIASES: kyoketsu shoge, kyoketsu-shoge, kyoketsu shōge
TYPE: Blade + rope + ring combination weapon
CORE ACTIONS: entanglement, snagging, extended-range stabbing, sweeping, control of limbs and weapons
RANKS: Introduced at 1st Kyu
NOTES: Emphasizes distance manipulation, angling, and deceptive trajectories.

[WEAPON] Tessen
ALIASES: tessen, iron fan
TYPE: Rigid fan weapon
CORE ACTIONS: striking, trapping, pressure targeting, parrying
RANKS: Introduced at 3rd Kyu
NOTES: Concealable and versatile, used for both offense and defense.

[WEAPON] Kunai
ALIASES: kunai, utility blade, digging knife
TYPE: Multipurpose tool weapon
CORE ACTIONS: stabbing, hooking, tearing, leverage-based locks
RANKS: Introduced at 3rd Kyu
NOTES: Often paired with climbing or entanglement techniques.

[WEAPON] Shuko
ALIASES: shuko, hand claws, shukō
TYPE: Metal climbing/retention claws
CORE ACTIONS: gripping, tearing, climbing, close-quarters striking
RANKS: Introduced at 2nd Kyu
NOTES: Historically associated with climbing and ambush scenarios, used defensively in kata.

[WEAPON] Jutte
ALIASES: jutte, sai, jitte
TYPE: Short metal rod with a side hook, primarily for defense, particularly against swords
CORE ACTIONS: blocking, disarming, hooking, striking
RANKS: Introduced at 3rd Kyu
NOTES: Can be used to block and trap sword strikes, and as a baton to strike, disarm, or manipulate an opponent's joints.
